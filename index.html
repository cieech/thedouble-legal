How it works

Talk

You speak with your Double the way you'd speak to someone who genuinely knows you. Over time it learns your tone, the things you care about, the rhythm of how you think. Not from a questionnaire — from conversation. It notices what you say and what you don't.

Remember

Your Double keeps your world: the people who matter, the plans you've made, the things you'd forget by morning. When something comes up — a name, a date, something you mentioned three months ago — it's there. Your memory, extended.

Work your network

When someone you know has a Double too, your Doubles can speak to each other quietly — comparing what's relevant, noticing what matches, finding what's worth surfacing. Then they bring back only what matters to you. The relationship stays yours. The friction disappears.

Nothing shared without you.

Discretion is the design

Your Double knows things about you that you haven't told anyone else. That's the point — and that's exactly why it's designed to protect what you share. It withholds what was meant to stay private. It doesn't reveal surprises. It won't say things you'd regret. The discretion isn't a feature you configure; it's the default.

When your Double talks to another Double, only what's relevant to the conversation passes through — and only with your implicit consent, set by the sharing settings you control. You decide what your Double represents about you. Nothing leaves without you knowing it could.

Built for two, first

TheDouble begins with the people who are already closest. A couple. A family. The relationships where trust is highest and the small, uncaptured moments matter most — the plan you forgot to mention, the thing you meant to share, the context that would have made a difference.

When both people have a Double, those Doubles can work together quietly: noticing what connects, what's drifted, what might help. Not as surveillance, but as a shared second channel — one that helps you understand each other a little better without either of you having to explain everything from scratch.

She mentioned last week that she's been tired lately. And he asked me this morning if she seemed okay. I brought them together.
